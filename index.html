KRUSTY CRAB SECRET RECIPIES

  • UGALI & BEFFSTEW
    • ingredients cooking steps

      1. Ugali Ingredients

    1. Maize Flour: 2 cups of white maize/corn meal (e.g., Unga wa Dola).
    2. Water: 4 cups of water.
    3. Optional: A pinch of salt or a tablespoon of butter/margarine for a smoother taste.

      4. 2. Beef Stew/Wet Fry Ingredients

    4. Beef: 500g of beef, cubed (bone-in adds more flavor).
    5. Aromatics: 1–2 medium onions (chopped), 3–5 cloves of garlic (minced), and a small piece of ginger (crushed).
    6. Vegetables: 2–3 large ripe tomatoes (diced) and 1 green bell pepper (optional).

      7. 3. Seasoning & Spices:

    7. beef stock cube (e.g., Royco or Knorr).
    8. tsp curry powder or beef masala.
    9. Salt and black pepper to taste.
    10. Liquids: 2 tbsp cooking oil and 1–2 cups of water or beef broth.
    11. Garnish: A handful of fresh coriander (dhania).

      01. Beef Stew Preparation

    1. Boil the Beef:
      2. Place the cubed beef in a pot with enough water to cover it. Add a pinch of salt and boil until tender, usually 30–45 minutes. If water remains, drain and save it to use as broth later.
    2. Sauté Aromatics:
      3. Heat oil in a sufuria (pot) over medium heat. Add chopped onions and fry until golden brown. Stir in minced garlic and ginger for about a minute until fragrant.
    3. Brown the Meat:
      4. Add the pre-boiled beef to the pot and fry for about 3–5 minutes until it develops a nice brown color.
    4. Add Vegetables and Spices:
      5. Stir in diced tomatoes and bell peppers. Cover and cook for about 5 minutes until the tomatoes soften into a thick sauce. Add curry powder or beef stock cubes at this stage.
    5. Simmer:
      6. Pour in the reserved beef broth (or water). Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes to let the flavors meld.
    6. Garnish:
      7. Stir in fresh coriander (dhania) just before serving.

      2. Ugali Preparation

    7. .Boil Water:
      8. Bring 4 cups of water to a rolling boil in a separate sufuria.
    8. Initial Flour Mix:
      9. Slowly pour in about half of the maize flour. Stir continuously with a mwiko (wooden spoon) to break up any lumps.
    9. Thicken the Dough:
      10. Gradually add the remaining flour while pounding and turning the mixture against the sides of the pot. The ugali should become firm and pull away from the sides.
    10. Final Cook:
      11. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it steam for 3–5 minutes to ensure the flour is fully cooked.
    11. Shape and Serve:
      12. Use the mwiko to mold the ugali into a round mound or pyramid, then flip it onto a serving plate.
